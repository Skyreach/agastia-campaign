## DM Notes

### Customizing Encounters

**Scale Difficulty:**
- **Easier:** Use non-combat options, reduce enemy numbers
- **Harder:** Add variations, increase enemy HP, combine encounters

**Tailor to Party:**
- Emphasize encounters matching PC interests
- Adjust loot to fill party needs
- Connect to ongoing quests when possible

**Make Encounters Memorable:**
- Give NPCs personalities (even monsters)
- Offer multiple solutions
- Include lasting consequences

### Random vs. Curated

**Roll Random When:**
- Party is traveling through familiar terrain
- You want to add unpredictability
- You need quick inspiration

**Choose Specific When:**
- Encounter serves story purpose
- Party deserves particular challenge/reward
- You want to foreshadow future events

### Encounter Frequency

**Recommended:**
- 1-2 encounters per day of travel (Tier 1)
- Not every encounter needs combat
- Balance action with story/exploration

### Weighted Results

**Lower rolls (2-5):** Trend toward helpful/neutral encounters
**Middle rolls (6-12):** Standard challenges with mixed options
**High rolls (13+):** Serious threats requiring tactical decisions

Each encounter page includes:
- **Core Scenario:** The basic encounter setup
- **Variations:** Ways to adjust difficulty or add complexity
- **Non-Combat Options:** Alternatives to fighting

Encounters are designed for 5 experienced players at levels 1-4. Adjust creature numbers (not types) for different party sizes.
