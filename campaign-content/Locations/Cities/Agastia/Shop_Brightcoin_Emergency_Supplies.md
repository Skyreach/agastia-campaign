# Brightcoin Emergency Supplies

## Overview

**Type & Specialty:** "Everything you need when you need it" - General adventuring supplies, equipment, and information services for those heading into danger

**Location:** [[Merchant District]] (Tier 4), strategically positioned near district's main gate leading to outer city

**Clientele:** Adventurers, travelers, merchants needing last-minute supplies, those seeking discreet information

**Reputation:** "Marcus always has what you need - even before you know you need it." Known for uncanny ability to stock exactly the right items and possess surprisingly useful information.

**Atmosphere:** Organized but dense. Every wall covered with hanging equipment, shelves packed with supplies, barrels of dry goods. Feels like walking into a perfectly curated catalog. Smell of leather, oil, dried herbs, and paper. Front area is standard shop; back room (visible through bead curtain) suggests more interesting inventory.

## Proprietor

### Marcus Brightcoin
**Role:** Proprietor, information broker, and remarkably well-connected merchant
**Faction:** None officially (Disposition: Carefully maintained neutrality)

**Background:**
- Tier 4 merchant who made fortune through combination of legitimate business and information trading
- Established Brightcoin Emergency Supplies fifteen years ago after retiring from caravan trade
- Traveled extensively in younger years, built network of contacts across the region
- Survived situations requiring both quick thinking and good equipment
- Reputation for discretion makes him trusted neutral party among multiple factions
- Provided party's starting equipment (remembers exactly what everyone purchased)

**Goals:**
- Short-term: Expand information network into [[Research Quarter]] and [[Noble Quarter]]
- Long-term: Become indispensable to all factions through information monopoly (without being controlled by any)

**Personality:**
- Affable and professional - makes everyone feel like valued customer
- Observant and analytical - notices details others miss
- Cautious risk-taker - takes calculated chances only
- Values reputation above profit (though profits handsomely anyway)
- Never forgets a face, transaction, or conversation

**Appearance:** Human male, late 40s, well-maintained appearance suggesting prosperity without ostentation. Practical clothing with many pockets. Wire-rimmed spectacles. Neat beard. Moves efficiently, wastes no motion. Eyes constantly tracking multiple things simultaneously.

**Interaction Hooks:**
- Would seek player help with: Recovering information assets (documents, informants in danger), investigating competitors' suspicious activities, delivering messages requiring deniability, protecting key informants
- Reluctance level: Willing but cautious (will involve party when he can maintain plausible deniability)
- Can offer players: Information (his primary currency), discounted supplies, connections to contacts throughout [[Agastia]], advance warning of threats, brokering meetings between factions

**Party Connections:**
- Potential bonds with: All party members (he outfitted them originally), especially [[Nikki]] (fellow merchant background, appreciates cleverness), [[Manny]] (respects practical mindset)
- Relationship type: Professional contact â†’ could evolve to Trusted ally / Information broker partnership

## Day-to-Day Operations

**Hours:**
- Full day (6am-8pm): Official business hours
- After hours (by arrangement): Information transactions, sensitive meetings
- Always available for "emergencies" (knock on back door, password required)

**Typical Day:**
- Early morning: Review overnight information from network, update inventory based on anticipated needs
- Morning: Busy with adventurers preparing for departure, routine sales
- Midday: Information consultations in back room, meetings with informants
- Afternoon: Restocking, correspondence with suppliers and information sources
- Evening: Discrete dealings, processing intelligence, planning next day's positioning
- Night: Encrypted correspondence, updating records in hidden ledger

**Regular Customers:**
- **Adventuring parties:** Last-minute supplies before expeditions (Marcus stocks what they forgot)
- **Travelers:** Emergency replacements for lost or damaged equipment
- **Merchants:** Caravan supplies, route information, market intelligence
- **Information seekers:** Those needing discrete intelligence (back room consultations)
- **Faction agents:** Using Marcus as neutral meeting ground or information broker

**Real Uses:**
- **Emergency Supplies:** Actually stocks items others run out of
- **Information Brokering:** Primary service - sells intelligence to all sides
- **Neutral Ground:** Back room serves as meeting place for sensitive negotiations
- **Message Service:** Delivers communications requiring discretion
- **Network Access:** Connects people who need to communicate without faction knowing
- **Early Warning:** Tips off valued customers about incoming threats

## Services & Inventory

### Primary Services

**Equipment Sales:**
- Standard adventuring gear (PHB prices)
- "Emergency markup" (10% premium for after-hours or urgent needs)
- Bulk discounts for regular customers (5-10% off)
- Layaway available for expensive items (requires 25% down)
- Will hold special orders (advance notice, 50% deposit)

**Information Services:**
- **Basic Information:** 5-25gp (publicly available but compiled conveniently)
  - District layouts, merchant schedules, faction public activities
  - Travel routes, weather forecasts, local rumors
- **Useful Intelligence:** 50-200gp (requires network access to gather)
  - Specific NPC locations and activities
  - Faction movements and plans (surface level)
  - Business opportunities, market trends
- **Sensitive Information:** 500-5,000gp (dangerous to acquire or reveal)
  - Faction secrets and internal conflicts
  - Political maneuvering, hidden alliances
  - Criminal activities, corruption evidence
  - Advance warning of raids, arrests, investigations

**Brokering Services:**
- **Message Delivery:** 10-100gp (depending on risk/discretion required)
- **Meeting Arrangement:** 50gp (neutral ground, Marcus ensures safety)
- **Introduction Service:** 100-500gp (connects client with otherwise inaccessible contacts)
- **Document Authentication:** 25gp (Marcus has seen forgeries, knows the real thing)

**Special Services:**
- **The Favor System:** Information too sensitive for money
  - Marcus extends credit for future reciprocal service
  - Accepts valuable information as payment
  - Creates web of obligations he manages carefully
- **The Emergency Kit:** Free for valued customers
  - Small package of essentials Marcus insists they take "just in case"
  - Always contains exactly what they'll need (suspiciously prescient)
  - Builds loyalty and demonstrates value

## DM Notes

### Plot Hooks

**The Missing Informant:**
- One of Marcus's key information sources disappeared
- Either captured, killed, or turned by rival faction
- Needs recovery or confirmation of fate
- Information they possessed dangerous if revealed
- Reward: Major favor from Marcus (valuable given his network), 500gp, priority information access
- Difficulty: Moderate (investigation, possible combat, faction politics)

**The Competing Information Broker:**
- New intelligence service undercutting Marcus's prices
- Either legitimate competition or front for faction's intelligence operation
- If faction-backed, threatens Marcus's neutrality (forces him to choose sides)
- Needs investigation of competitor's backers and methods
- Reward: Permanent 25% discount on information, introduction to Marcus's highest-level contacts, partnership offer
- Difficulty: Moderate-High (investigation, possible faction confrontation)

**The Dangerous Knowledge:**
- Marcus acquired information too sensitive to sell or keep safely
- Multiple factions would kill to obtain or suppress it
- Needs trusted agents to either secure it somewhere safe or use it to defuse situation
- Possession of knowledge endangers everyone who knows it
- Reward: Significant payment (2,000gp+), major faction favor, or incredibly valuable counter-intelligence
- Difficulty: High (multiple factions involved, extreme danger)

**The Party's Starting Equipment:**
- Marcus "randomly" stocked exactly what party needed for first adventure
- How did he know? Investigating reveals he received anonymous tip
- Tip came from someone with precognition or deep insider knowledge
- Following this thread reveals larger conspiracy
- Reward: Understanding of forces manipulating events, potential ally revelation
- Difficulty: Variable (depends on who sent tip and why)

### Personality Quirks

- Never asks direct questions - lets people volunteer information
- Keeps small notebook, makes cryptic notes during conversations
- Offers tea during consultations (excellent listener, poor tea)
- Refers to information as "inventory" and "stock" (discusses it like merchandise)
- Never confirms or denies specific knowledge - implies without stating
- Remembers every purchase anyone makes (uses for profiling and predictions)
- Maintains perfect poker face except rare genuine smile for trusted friends

### Secret Information

**The True Business Model:**
- Shop breaks even or slight loss
- Real profit from information brokering (10x merchandise income)
- Uses shop as cover and recruitment source for informant network
- Every customer potentially becomes information asset
- Legitimate business provides plausible deniability

**The Network:**
- 50+ active informants throughout [[Agastia]] (all tiers)
- Includes: Servants in noble houses, dock workers, council clerks, temple acolytes, criminals
- Marcus never reveals sources (absolute confidentiality maintains network)
- Compartmentalized - informants don't know each other
- Some informants don't realize they're providing intelligence (casual conversations Marcus mines)

**The Insurance Files:**
- Hidden archives contain blackmail material on important figures
- Not actively used for extortion (would destroy neutral reputation)
- Insurance against being silenced or eliminated
- Dead man's switch: Files release if Marcus dies suspiciously
- Several factions aware of files' existence (deters violence against him)

**The [[Chaos Cult]] Problem:**
- Marcus suspects cult infiltration but can't confirm
- Some information he's sold likely aided cult operations (unknowingly)
- Wrestling with moral implications of neutral stance
- If party trustworthy, might recruit them to investigate his own network
- Fears discovering he's compromised, but needs to know

**Personal History:**
- "Brightcoin" not his birth name (changed it for merchant career)
- Original background more interesting than claims
- Possibly noble-born fallen from status (would explain connections and education)
- Or former spy/intelligence agent (would explain skills and network)
- Carefully guards true history (vulnerability if revealed)

### Integration with Campaign

**Immediate Utility:**
- Party already knows Marcus (bought starting equipment from him)
- Comfortable returning for resupply
- Natural introduction to information brokering
- Can provide rumors, hooks, quest leads

**Progressive Relationship:**
- Initially: Helpful merchant who seems oddly informed
- Early partnership: Realizes party useful for information gathering
- Established: Shares intelligence, involves in sensitive operations
- Deep trust: Reveals aspects of network, true scope of knowledge
- Crisis: Party must protect Marcus or his network from threat

**Information Economy:**
- Party can sell information to Marcus (payment or credit)
- Marcus trades information for party's services
- Creates two-way relationship beyond merchant/customer
- Party becomes part of network (valuable but potentially compromising)

**Faction Complexity:**
- Marcus's neutrality creates tension - factions pressure him
- Party's relationship with Marcus affects faction standing
- Being seen entering back room signals involvement in intelligence world
- Other factions may try using party to spy on or compromise Marcus

**[[Chaos Cult]] Revelation:**
- Eventually discovering Marcus unknowingly aided cult
- How does he react? Maintains neutrality or takes sides?
- Party can help him identify cult infiltration in network
- His information assets valuable in anti-cult operations
- But using them abandons neutrality (existential crisis for character)

**The Emergency Kit Prophecy:**
- Marcus's "lucky guesses" about needed supplies too accurate
- Either he has precognitive source or is receiving tips from time-traveler/diviner
- Investigation reveals he's pawn in larger game
- Or: He's more than he appears (secret diviner? Subtle mind-reader?)
- Truth adds layer to character, campaign mysteries
