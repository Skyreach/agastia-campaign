# Working-Class Armory

## Overview

**Type & Specialty:** Mundane weapons and armor - practical, affordable equipment for laborers, guards, and working folk who need reliable protection without the price tag

**Location:** Lower Residential District (Tier 5), corner shop near the worker's market, ground floor of a converted tenement

**Clientele:** Factory workers, dock laborers, caravan guards, militia volunteers, struggling adventurers, local families

**Reputation:** "Honest prices for honest folk." Known for fair dealing, affordable repairs, and never turning away someone in genuine need. The community's armory.

**Atmosphere:** Cluttered but organized workshop. Smell of coal forge, leather oil, and steel. Tools hang on pegboards. Armor stands display simple chainmail and leather. Weapon racks hold practical blades - no fancy decorations. Sound of hammer on anvil from the back. Feels like a neighbor's workshop, not a fancy store.

## Proprietor

**Torvin Hammerstead** - Human male, late 40s, former caravan guard (Retired Fighter)

**Personality:**
- Salt-of-the-earth honest - wouldn't cheat you even if he could
- Community-focused - knows his neighbors by name, their kids, their struggles
- Practical to the bone - "Flashy gets you killed. Functional keeps you breathing."
- Generous with credit for locals he trusts

**Appearance:** Stocky build with a slight limp (old leg wound), close-cropped graying hair, leather apron stained from forge work, calloused hands, warm eyes. Speaks plainly. No pretense. Wears simple working clothes reinforced at the elbows.

**Motivation:** Grew up poor in this district. Made enough coin as a guard to open a shop serving the people who raised him. Believes everyone deserves a chance to defend themselves and their family, regardless of coin purse. Takes pride in keeping the working folk equipped.

**Quest Hook - The Protection Money:**
- **Need:** A gang calling themselves the "Iron Thumb" has been pressuring Lower District shopkeepers for "protection fees." Torvin refuses to pay - he won't fund thugs terrorizing his neighbors. They've threatened to torch his shop if he doesn't comply by week's end.
- **Problem:** Torvin could use help dealing with the gang. He won't pay, but he also can't afford to lose everything. Local militia is stretched thin and slow to respond. The gang operates out of an abandoned warehouse near the docks.
- **Reward:** In exchange for handling the Iron Thumb problem:
  - Permanent discount on all equipment (30% off)
  - Free repairs and sharpening for life
  - Emergency loans extended with no interest
  - Torvin spreads word among the working community - reputation boost in Lower District
  - Access to "neighborhood watch" - locals willing to share information and small favors
- **Difficulty:** Moderate - Gang has 8-12 members, mix of thugs and desperate folks forced into service. Confrontation likely, but investigation might reveal who's really pulling strings. Locals will help if they believe the gang can be stopped.

## Day-to-Day Operations

**Hours:**
- Dawn to dusk (6am-8pm): Open for business
- Early mornings busiest (workers heading to jobs, buying quick repairs)
- Evenings steady (folk coming home, getting gear fixed before next shift)

**Typical Day:**
- Morning: Laborers buying cheap weapons for protection, parents getting knives for sons/daughters starting apprenticeships
- Midday: Repairs pile up - damaged tools, bent blades, torn straps
- Afternoon: Forge work - fixing broken equipment, sharpening tools
- Evening: Regulars stop by to chat, share neighborhood news, pick up repairs

**Regular Customers:**
- **Dock workers:** Replacing worn tools, buying protection against harbor rats (literal and criminal)
- **Factory laborers:** Cheap daggers, simple armor for walking home through rough areas
- **Militia volunteers:** Affordable equipment when city stipend doesn't cover it
- **Struggling adventurers:** First weapon on credit, last-ditch repairs before a job
- **Local families:** Outfitting children, maintaining household weapons

**Real Uses:**
- **Affordable Protection:** Working folk can actually afford basic weapons and armor
- **Community Credit:** Torvin extends loans to locals he knows - pay when you can
- **Quick Repairs:** Get your blade fixed during lunch break, pick it up after shift
- **Local News Hub:** Regulars share neighborhood happenings, job opportunities, warnings
- **Emergency Equipment:** Someone in trouble can get armed fast, pay later

## Services & Inventory

**Primary Services:**

**Equipment Sales:**
- Standard PHB weapons and armor at normal prices
- "Good enough" quality - functional but not fancy (25% break chance on critical fumble)
- Payment plans available for trusted regulars (10% down, rest in installments)

**Repairs & Maintenance:**
- Weapon repairs: Half cost of buying new (5gp for a longsword repair vs 15gp new)
- Armor repairs: Half cost of buying new
- Sharpening: 1 sp (+1 damage for next combat only)
- Basic modifications: 5-10gp (add spikes to gauntlets, reinforce straps, etc.)

**Community Services:**
- Emergency loans: For locals in good standing, Torvin extends credit with no interest
- Free sharpening for militia volunteers
- Will hold valuable repairs as collateral for loans

- **Apprentice Work:** 10-15 gp - Items made by Torvin's apprentices, slightly discounted, quality varies (DM rolls for minor quirks - cosmetic only)
- **Militia Surplus:** Variable pricing - When local militia upgrades, old equipment sold cheap (50-75% normal price), limited quantity
- **Custom Work:** Available on commission, 2-3 week wait, +10% cost but Torvin ensures quality

**Pricing:** PHB standard prices. No markup, no games. Torvin operates on thin margins and makes it up in volume and community goodwill.

**Quality Warning:**
"Good enough" quality means items have a 25% chance to break on a critical fumble (nat 1 in combat). Weapon becomes damaged (disadvantage on attacks) or armor loses 1 AC until repaired. Repairs cost half the item's value. This is the trade-off for affordable prices.

## Connections

**District Integration:**
- Anchor of the Lower Residential community - everyone knows Torvin
- Informally partnered with local workers' collective (provides equipment, they send business)
- Respected by Merit Council for keeping district armed against crime
- Tolerated by higher-tier smiths (serves different market, not competition)

**Related NPCs:**
- **Dock workers:** Regular customers, source of salvaged materials and maritime job rumors
- **Factory foremen:** Send workers to Torvin for safety equipment
- **Militia captain:** Appreciates Torvin equipping volunteers, sometimes tips him off about surplus sales
- **Local fence:** Torvin refuses stolen goods, but knows who to report suspicious sellers to
- **Apprentices:** Two young locals learning the trade, sometimes produce items for sale

**Faction Ties:**
- **Workers' Collective:** Informal alliance - Torvin gives discounts, they recommend his shop
- **Merit Council:** Neutral respect - appreciates community service, concerned about "good enough" quality
- **Dispossessed:** Sympathetic - has hired reformed gang members as apprentices, provides equipment on credit
- **Iron Thumb Gang:** HOSTILE - refuses to pay protection money, actively resisting

## DM Notes

**Plot Hooks:**
- Essential equipment source for low-level parties starting in Lower District
- Iron Thumb protection racket provides immediate quest
- Community connections give party "in" with working folk
- Torvin hears street-level rumors (missing workers, strange shipments, gang movements)
- Apprentices might get in trouble, need rescue/help

**Personality Quirks:**
- Always offers tea to customers while they wait for repairs
- Keeps ledger of who owes what - never forgets a debt or a favor
- Tests weapons personally before selling ("If I wouldn't trust it, you shouldn't either")
- Has story about every scar - loves to share while working forge
- Soft spot for kids - gives free daggers to 12-year-olds starting apprenticeships

**Mechanical Use:**
- Affordable starting equipment for broke parties
- Repair service cheaper than buying new (important for low-level economy)
- Sharpening service gives temporary damage boost for tough fights
- "Good enough" quality creates tension - save money or risk equipment failure?
- Emergency loans enable plot progression when party is cash-strapped

**Connection to Campaigns:**
- Lower District anchor - party's first contact with working-class community
- Gateway to workers' collective faction
- Source of street-level rumors and information
- Provides equipment with meaningful trade-offs (cheap but breakable)
- Iron Thumb quest introduces gang/crime storylines

**Economic Realism:**
- Torvin operates on 10-15% margins, barely above break-even
- Makes real profit on repairs (labor costs low when you do it yourself)
- Community credit creates loyalty but cash flow problems
- Struggles during economic downturns in Lower District
- Could be forced to close if major disaster (fire, protection racket, etc.)

**Character Development Potential:**
- If party helps with Iron Thumb, Torvin becomes loyal ally
- Might ask party to escort shipment, investigate supplier, rescue apprentice
- Could eventually expand into community armory/training center if party invests
- Retirement arc: Train successor, pass shop to trusted apprentice
- Betrayal potential: If Iron Thumb threatens his family, might be forced to cooperate against party

## Toggle: Lower District Context

**Why This Shop Matters:**
Most working folk can't afford The Wanderer's Pack or Blades of Glory. They need basic protection - a knife to walk home safely, cheap chainmail for militia duty, a repaired sword that'll last another season. Torvin fills that gap. He's not getting rich, but he's keeping his neighbors alive.

**Social Function:**
The shop is more than commerce - it's a community hub. Workers stop by after shifts, share news, warn each other about dangers. Torvin knows who's struggling, who needs help, who can be trusted. He's the neighborhood's quiet guardian, arming them against both literal and economic threats.

**Contrast to Higher Tiers:**
- **Tier 1-3:** Magic items, enchantments, prestige - inaccessible to common folk
- **Tier 4:** Quality guaranteed, but prices assume adventurer income
- **Tier 5:** "Good enough" quality at prices working folk can afford
- **Tier 6-7:** Cheaper, but less reliable/more dangerous sources

Torvin occupies the sweet spot: affordable, honest, community-focused. Not fancy, but functional. Not perfect, but good enough to keep food on the table and family safe.
