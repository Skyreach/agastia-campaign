## üéØ THE STARFALL ANCHORS (Smuggled Artifacts)

### What They Are
**Starfall Anchors** - Crystalline fragments from a Feywild star that fell centuries ago, forged into dual-purpose artifacts:
- **Primary function:** Planar barrier anchors (stabilize Material/Feywild boundary)
- **Dormant function:** Wish-granting prisms (suppressed when properly installed in Feywild)

### Physical Description
- 18-inch crystalline pylons, six-sided, tapering to points
- Deep purple core with silver veins that pulse with starlight
- Surface etched with flowing Sylvan script (True Names)
- Cold to touch, makes reality "shimmer" within 5ft
- Hum at frequency just below hearing

### What They're FOR (Focus for Clues)
- **Legitimate use:** Augury commissioned them for planar barrier research (Project Threshold)
- **Defensive research:** Studying how to improve Agastia's protective wards
- **Extremely rare:** Lord Zaos guards his vault carefully
- **Diplomatic loan:** Sensitive Feywild transaction
- **Confidential:** Merit Council keeps this secret (public doesn't know)

### The Confidential Shipment
- **Manifest listing:** "Research Materials - Class 7 Restricted" (vague, coded)
- **Actual contents:** 6 Starfall Anchors from Lord Zaos's vault
- **Declared value:** Not publicly listed (confidential)
- **True value:** 30,000 gp research grant + diplomatic significance
- **Consignee:** The Augury, Scholar Quarter, Arcane Research Division
- **Project name:** "Project Threshold - Defensive Ward Enhancement Study"

### Stakes (Session Scope - 1-2 Sessions)
- **Chaos Cult:** Wants anchors for dimensional corruption ritual (4 anchors already in their hands)
- **Legitimate research blocked:** Augury can't complete critical ward improvements
- **Political catastrophe:** If Castle finds out Augury lost Feywild diplomatic loan
- **Lord Zaos (Kyle's patron):** Furious, demands return, potential diplomatic incident
- **Immediate danger:** Cult has 4, needs final 2 to complete Phase 2 of ritual

## üìê USER'S MYSTERY DESIGN RULES (To Follow for Skill)

### Core Principles

**1. Fantasy Setting Requirement:**
- Make it fantastical and matter!
- Not mundane goods or low stakes
- Consequences if unchecked should be significant

**2. 3-Clue Rule:**
- Each conclusion needs 3 independent clues
- Each clue must be sufficient ALONE to reach conclusion
- No "combine 3 partial clues" design (that's 3 conclusions, not 1)
- Example BAD: "6ft tall" + "green sweater" + "gray hair" (only works if unique on ALL)
- Example GOOD: "Signed confession" OR "Witnessed act" OR "Unique tool at scene"

**3. No Red Herrings:**
- User says "overrated" - don't use them
- Focus on multiple paths to truth, not false paths

**4. Proactive Clues (Failsafe):**
- If players stuck, villain acts
- New crime scene (another victim, robbery)
- Retaliation (thugs sent after party)
- Clue delivered (matchbook, letter, witness comes forward)
- Something semantically appropriate to the mystery

**5. Two Clue Types:**
- **Revelations:** Answer who/what/why (solve the mystery)
- **Leads:** Point to next location (continue investigation)
- Both follow 3-clue rule but in different ways
- Early scenes may have only leads (no revelations yet)
- Deep investigation eventually reveals truth

**6. Player Agency:**
- No "difficulty scaling" that changes mystery based on level
- Players choose their own path
- Multiple solutions valid

### 3-3-3-1 Structure

**3 Entry Points:**
- Different triggers to start investigation
- Each viable on its own
- Don't let one entry point "moot" others

**3 Investigation Locations (minimum):**
- Each entry point should lead to ~3 investigation sites
- Locations can overlap between entries (interconnected web)
- Flexible: Can add more if players re-route

**3 Clues Per Location/Conclusion:**
- Minimum 3 clues per revelation
- 2 extras serve as backups if first doesn't survive players
- Additional clues reinforce suspicions (desirable)

**1 Final Revelation:**
- All paths converge to same truth
- Players can solve with any 3-4 well-chosen nodes

### Depth Gauge

**Ask: "How many successful leads should players follow?"**
- Typical: 2-3 leads before confrontation
- For Geist Investigation: 2-3 leads confirmed
- Don't short-circuit flow
- Forgive PC re-routing (can add leads if needed)

### Mystery Design Process

**Step 1: Understand the Situation**
- Who is the villain/culprit?
- How did they commit the crime?
- Why did they do it?
- When did it happen?
- Where did it occur?

**Step 2: Create Revelation List**
- List all conclusions PCs need to reach
- For each revelation, design 3 independent clues
- Each clue must support conclusion alone and in full

**Step 3: Create Location List**
- List all places PCs can investigate
- For each location, provide 4-5 clues
- Mix of revelations (solve mystery) and leads (next location)

**Step 4: Design Entry Points**
- 3 different ways to trigger investigation
- Each points to different starting locations
- Ensure balance (no single entry moots others)

**Step 5: Map Clue Flow**
- Create diagrams showing entry ‚Üí investigation ‚Üí revelation
- Verify redundancy (3+ clues per revelation)
- Check depth (2-3 leads to confrontation)
- Plan proactive clues if players stuck

**Step 6: Test Flexibility**
- Can players reach truth via different paths?
- Does mystery work if they skip locations?
- Are there enough leads to keep investigating?
- Failsafes in place if stuck?
