## üèõÔ∏è TIER STRUCTURE DEFINITION (Completed 2025-12-13)

### Tier Hierarchy (1 = Highest, 7 = Lowest)

**Tier 1: The Castle**
- Most protected part of the city
- Merit Council's inner sanctum
- Highest security and prestige

**Tiers 2-3: Highest Merit Citizens**
- Split between two functional zones:
  - Research facilities & augury (magical/academic elite)
  - Noble residences (political/wealth elite)
- **Key Establishment:** High-caliber equipment shop
  - Selected: **Blades of Glory** (from Enchanting Emporiums)
  - Premium magic weapons/armor
  - Signature item: Blade of Glory (adaptable/shapeshifting sword - Very Rare, 25,000gp)
  - Features: Exclusive guild hall, master enchanters, highest quality craftsmanship

**Tier 4: The Masses' Ceiling**
- **Merchant District** - Commercial heart, trade, shops
- **Scholar Quarter** - Education, libraries, testing centers
- Highest tier average citizens can access without special circumstances
- Standard residential for "up-and-coming" citizens
- Some areas bleed into Tier 5 (sketchier sections)
- Theme: Earned merit, social mobility, aspiration

**Tier 5: Lower Merit / Transitional**
- **Lower Residential District** - Working class housing
- Overlap with Tier 4's seedier areas
- Citizens with lower merit status but still employed
- Theme: Honest work, modest living

**Tier 6: Lost Status (Recovering)**
- **Dock District** - Maritime workers, cargo handlers
- Those who've lost their merit status but still working
- Some recovering, seeking to regain status
- Theme: Fallen but fighting

**Tier 7: Lost Status (Given Up)**
- **The Depths** - Lowest level, underground
- The Dispossessed
- Those who've lost status and stopped trying to regain it
- Theme: Survival, desperation, black markets

### Key Design Principle
**Not many peasants in Agastia** - This is a meritocracy. Citizens either:
- Have earned their place (Tiers 1-4)
- Are working class maintaining status (Tier 5)
- Have fallen from status (Tiers 6-7)

But there's no "peasant underclass" - everyone had to prove merit to live here or fell from it.

### 1. EQUIPMENT & ARMORIES (Weapons/Armor)

**Tier 2-3: Blades of Glory (Premium Magic Shop)**
- **Availability:** Very Rare & Legendary magic items
- **Examples:**
  - Blade of Glory (Very Rare, 25,000gp) - adaptable weapon, +1, can attach objects to hilt
  - Armor of Invulnerability (90,000gp)
  - Rod of Lordly Might (75,000gp)
- **Services:**
  - Master enchanters (permanent enchantments)
  - Custom weapon crafting (specify properties)
  - Item identification (automatically succeeds)
  - Repair/restoration of legendary items
- **Mechanical Benefits:**
  - Access to items that fundamentally change capabilities
  - Can commission specific enchantments (at DM discretion, very expensive)
  - Guild membership provides benefits (see Blades of Glory details)
- **Restrictions:**
  - Must be invited or prove worth (completed major quest, level 10+, or recommendation)
  - Minimum 1,000gp purchase to browse
- **Price Range:** 1,000gp - 90,000gp+

**Tier 4: The Wanderer's Pack & General Merchants (Adventuring Supplies)**
- **Availability:** Common, Uncommon, occasionally Rare magic items
- **Examples:**
  - +1 Weapons (Uncommon, 500-1,000gp)
  - Potions of Healing (Common, 50gp)
  - Rope of Climbing (Uncommon, 500gp)
  - Bag of Holding (Uncommon, 500gp)
- **Services:**
  - Standard item identification (Arcana check required)
  - Minor enchantments (+1 weapons/armor only)
  - Repairs for mundane and common magic items
  - Gear advice from experienced adventurers
- **Mechanical Benefits:**
  - Reliable access to adventuring essentials
  - Quality guarantee (Kessa Ironfoot tests all rope personally)
  - 20% discount for return customers (quest reward)
- **Restrictions:** None, public access
- **Price Range:** 1gp - 5,000gp (standard PHB/DMG prices)

**Tier 5: Working-Class Armory (Basic Weapons & Repairs)**
- **Availability:** Mundane weapons/armor, no magic items
- **Examples:**
  - Standard longsword (15gp)
  - Chain mail (75gp)
  - Crossbow bolts (20 for 1gp)
  - Basic adventuring gear (rope, torches, etc.)
- **Services:**
  - Weapon/armor repairs (half cost of new)
  - Sharpening (1sp, +1 to damage for next combat only)
  - Basic modifications (spike knuckles on gauntlets, etc.)
- **Mechanical Benefits:**
  - Cheap repairs (50% of tier 4 prices)
  - "Good enough" quality (25% chance item breaks on critical fumble)
  - Emergency loans (shopkeeper knows local workers, will extend credit)
- **Restrictions:** No magic item services
- **Price Range:** 1sp - 200gp

**Tier 6: Dock Armory (Maritime Weapons & Salvage)**
- **Availability:** Mundane weapons (focus on naval/maritime), salvaged gear
- **Examples:**
  - Harpoons (5gp, 1d6 piercing, thrown 20/60)
  - Boarding axes (10gp, as handaxe with hook)
  - Salvaged armor (half price, cosmetically damaged but functional)
  - Nets, grappling hooks, maritime tools
- **Services:**
  - Waterproofing equipment (10gp, items resist water damage)
  - Repairs using salvaged parts (1/3 cost, takes 1d4 days)
  - "No questions asked" purchases (black market connections)
- **Mechanical Benefits:**
  - Specialist maritime equipment unavailable elsewhere
  - Salvage sales (buy damaged goods cheap: 25-50% price, DM rolls for hidden flaws)
  - Fence services (sell stolen goods for 25% value, no questions)
- **Restrictions:** Limited selection (DM rolls stock availability)
- **Price Range:** 1gp - 100gp

**Tier 7: Black Market Armory (Illegal & Stolen Goods)**
- **Availability:** Stolen goods, illegal weapons, questionable quality
- **Examples:**
  - "Liberated" +1 sword (250gp, no questions about origin)
  - Poisoned weapons (illegal in Agastia, 50gp for 3 doses)
  - Sharpened chain (improvised weapon, 5gp, 1d6 slashing)
  - Broken magic items (25gp, might work if repaired...)
- **Services:**
  - No services, cash and carry only
  - Will buy anything, no questions (10% of value)
  - Information about guard patrols (for a price)
- **Mechanical Benefits:**
  - Access to illegal items (poison, concealed weapons)
  - Cheap "maybe magic" items (DM rolls: 50% chance item is fake/broken)
  - Stolen goods buyback (offload hot items)
- **Restrictions:**
  - Must know someone who knows someone (Charisma check to find)
  - Being caught shopping here = crime (fines, possible arrest)
  - No guarantees on quality (buyer beware)
- **Price Range:** 1sp - 500gp (prices vary wildly, haggling expected)

## üë• NPC TRACKING REQUIREMENTS

**Added:** 2025-12-14 (per user request)

For each NPC created in establishment files, we must track:

### Required NPC Information

**1. Faction Affiliation**
- Which faction do they represent or align with?
- Options: Merit Council, Chaos Cult, Dispossessed, Decimate Project, Merchant Guilds, None, Multiple
- Note if affiliation is secret or public knowledge

**2. Disposition Toward Faction**
- How do they feel about their affiliated faction?
- Options: Loyal, Conflicted, Using them, Being used, Secretly opposed, Genuinely aligned
- Include nuance - not all faction members are true believers

**3. Personal Goals & Interests**
- What does this NPC want?
- Both short-term (practical) and long-term (aspirational)
- What motivates their daily actions?
- What would make them take risks?

**4. Player Interaction Hooks**
- Would they ask players for help? Under what circumstances?
- Are they reluctant to involve outsiders, or eager for assistance?
- What could players offer them (besides money)?
- What information or resources could they provide players?

**5. Relationship Potential**
- Which party members might connect with this NPC?
- What shared interests, backgrounds, or goals exist?
- Potential for rivalry, friendship, mentorship, romance?
- How do their personalities mesh or clash with PCs?

### Implementation in Files

When creating NPC sections in establishment files, include:

```markdown
### [NPC Name]
**Role:** [Proprietor/Employee/Regular Customer/etc.]
**Faction:** [Faction name] (Disposition: [loyal/conflicted/etc.])

**Background:** [Brief history]

**Goals:**
- Short-term: [Immediate practical goal]
- Long-term: [Aspirational goal]

**Personality:** [Key traits, mannerisms]

**Interaction Hooks:**
- Would seek player help with: [Specific problems/needs]
- Reluctance level: [Eager/Willing/Reluctant/Desperate-only]
- Can offer players: [Resources, information, services]

**Party Connections:**
- Potential bonds with: [Specific PC names and why]
- Relationship type: [Ally/Rival/Mentor/Contact/etc.]
```

This ensures every NPC becomes a living character with agency and story potential, not just a service provider.
